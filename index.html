A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, often referred to as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist, engineer, and statesman who played a crucial role in India's space and defense programs. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Kalam is best known for his contributions to India's space and missile development programs. He played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and the indigenous nuclear-capable ballistic missile systems. He served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and as the President of India from 2002 to 2007. Dr. Kalam was popularly known as the "Missile Man of India" due to his significant contributions to the country's missile development programs. He was a strong advocate for science and technology education, particularly among the youth, and often interacted with students to inspire and encourage them to pursue careers in scientific research and development.
